Chilli Con Carne

Description

If I have time on a Sunday and have a long time in which I can cook, Chilli (Chile?) Con Carne is my go-to.

Most people, where I am from in the UK, anyway, think of this as a Mexican dish, but its history is more complicated than that. It's a Tex-Mex dish, originating from Mexican immigrants in the US, cooking for locals with the ingredients they were able to acquire locally.

Most recipes call for minced beef. Don't do it! Get a nice big slab of beef that you can cut into chunks that will fall apart when cooked for a long time. Also, a proper chilli sauce made from proper dried chillis is essential. Don't half-arse this - your tastebuds will thank you.

Ingredients

  • 2-2.5 lbs. chuck roast (or alternative cut)
  • 3 Ancho dried chillies
  • 3 Guajillo dried chillies
  • 1 Onion
  • 4 Garlic cloves
  • 3-4 Roma tomatoes (or can of whole tomatoes)
  • 1 Chipotle in adobo (optional)
  • 1-2 Cans beans (I use kidney)
  • 5 Cups stock (ideally beef)
  • 1 Tablespoon Mexican oregano
  • 1/2 Teaspoon cumin
  • 1 Teaspoon salt (plus more to taste)
  • Freshly cracked black pepper
  • Optional but recommended: Dark chocolate, ground coffee and/or worcestershire sauce
