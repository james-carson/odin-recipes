Welsh Rarebit

Description

Welsh Rarebit has nothing to do with rabbit, or potentially even Wales (careful), but is instead a simple, tasty recipe that is essentially posh cheese on toast

If you're going to follow the recipe properly, then you are going to need to put beer in it (does that make it Welsh?), but this is a breakfast, brucnh or lunch recipe and who's cracking open a tinnie at 10am? The Welsh?

Personally, I make it with a splash of milk, rather than beer, which still makes it taste beautiful. Don't skimp on the cheese, or even the bread, as this simple dish is all about high-quality ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 120ml brown ale (or milk, as stated above)
  • 25g unsalted butter
  • 25g plain flour
  • 140g mature cheddar, coarsely grated
  • 1 heaped tsp English mustard powder
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tbsp chopped chives
  • 2 large slices sourdough bread

Steps

  1. In a small saucepan gently warm the ale, set aside.
  2. Take another small saucepan and over a medium heat melt the butter until it begins to foam, tip in the flour and stir everything together, cooking for 1 min.
  3. Whisk in the warm ale in several additions to create a thick sauce then whisk through the cheese to create a thick paste.
  4. Stir the mustard, Worcestershire and chives through the sauce.
  5. Heat a grill to its highest setting. Place the slices of bread on a flat baking sheet and grill on one side until golden brown.
  6. Flip the bread over and spread each one with the cheese mixture.
  7. Place back under the grill and cook for a further 1-2 mins or until golden brown and bubbling, serve immediately.
